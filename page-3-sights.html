Astor Place

Near the entrances to the 6, N, and W lines of the subway, Astor Place the gateway for people coming to the East Village.
That is, until Second Avenue Subway is extended down here (expected completion in 2315).
What to see: mix of traditional and modern architecture, historic Cooper Union university, people-watching

St. Marks

The heart of the East Village, full of shops, restaurants, and people meandering the narrow sidewalks.
A vibrant scene, even if some feel the true St. Marks has died.
What to see: yummy restaurants, cozy bars, unique shops

Tompkins Square Park

Tompkins Square is much needed pocket of green (albeit highly manicured green) in this dense, urban neighborhood.
Also an important part of EV history, being the setting for the Tompkins Square Riot of 1988.
What to see: cute dogs playing in the dog park, night time movies in the summer, farmers market on Sundays
